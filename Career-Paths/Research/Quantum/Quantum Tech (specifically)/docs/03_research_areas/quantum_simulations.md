# Quantum Simulation

Quantum simulation uses quantum systems to model and study other quantum systems that are difficult to simulate on classical computers.

This research area was one of the original motivations for quantum computing and remains one of its most promising near-term applications.

---

## What is quantum simulation?

Quantum simulation involves:
- Mapping a target quantum system to a controllable quantum device
- Studying its dynamics, ground states, or properties
- Extracting physical insights not accessible classically

Simulators may be digital (gate-based) or analog.

---

## Why quantum simulation matters

Many important problems in:
- Condensed matter physics
- Quantum chemistry
- High-energy physics
- Materials science

are computationally intractable on classical computers.

Quantum simulators offer a path to understanding:
- Strongly correlated systems
- Phase transitions
- Molecular energy landscapes
- Exotic quantum states

---

## Types of quantum simulation

### Digital quantum simulation
- Uses quantum circuits
- Flexible but resource-intensive
- Requires error mitigation or correction

### Analog quantum simulation
- Uses specially engineered quantum systems
- Highly efficient for specific models
- Less programmable

Both approaches are actively researched.

## Student project ideas

- Simulate spin chains
- Implement Trotterized time evolution
- Study ground states of small molecules
- Compare digital and analog simulation approaches
- Analyze noise effects on simulation accuracy

Projects should emphasize physical interpretation.

Quantum simulation specialists often work in:
- Academic research groups

## Who should choose this area?

This track is ideal if you:
- Enjoy physics-heavy problems
- Like interpreting simulation results
- Are interested in materials or chemistry
- Prefer conceptual depth over pure abstraction
